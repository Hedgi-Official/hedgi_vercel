Problem
Currency fluctuation is a concern for all companies operating cross-border in some way. In 2025, a MillTechFX poll of 750 finance officers found 81 percent of companies hedge their currency exposure. This indicates that companies do hedge their exposures, but far from everyone is. The same survey showed that 75 percent of companies not hedging lose money due to currency fluctuations — among inhedged firms,  77% of U.S. corporates, 75% of UK companies, and 72% of European companies were adversely affected by exchange rate moves. 

Continuing, MillTechFX highlights how 62 percent of companies plan to increase the extent of FX coverage and duration as a result of geopolitical uncertainty. Further, 52 percent of companies that never hedged before are now considering it, as inaction is exposing costs. 

In more detail, a Kyribias Q1 report 2024 indicated that among 1700 public traded companies, currency moves erased a combined $9.83 billion from North American and European corporate earnings. This is a 44.6 percent increase in losses due to FX movements from previous quarters. 

A Nordea bank study focusing on the Nordics found that “too many” SMEs are underhedges against currency fluctuations. The study highlighted that despite suffering major losses during COVID-19, many companies had taken little action to hedge their assets and, as a result, feared cash illiquidity. 

The reasons SMEs shy away from currency risk management are telling. According to the Nordea study, lack of in-house expertise and lack of time are the top barriers preventing small firms from hedging properly. Unlike a Fortune 500 company, a 50-person import/export business might not have a CFO versed in derivatives or the bandwidth to constantly monitor FX markets. There is also a perception that hedging is expensive or complicated. In the global MillTechFX survey, 50% of companies that don’t hedge said they choose not to because it’s “too expensive.” This cost concern is especially acute for smaller enterprises, which may find bank hedging products (forwards, options) come with fees, collateral requirements, or minimum sizes that feel out of reach. As a result, SMEs are left more vulnerable to FX movements than their larger peers.
 
The problem of FX fluctuation is amplified in emerging markets, where currencies can be more volatile. Hedgi’s initial focus includes countries like Brazil, Mexico, and India, which have seen significant currency swings impacting businesses. For example, in mid-2024, the Brazilian real was the worst-performing major emerging currency, plunging about 7% in one month amid fiscal and policy worries. Such a sharp move can wreak havoc on Brazilian importers (who suddenly face higher costs for overseas goods) and on exporters’ pricing strategies. 


Solution 
Hedgi provides SMEs with “currency insurance,” a simple way for any business to protect itself against FX swings. Our product is built on radical simplicity: the user only inputs the amount and time period, and Hedgi automatically executes the hedge.
No jargon, no barriers. Users never see complex terms like swap rates, margin requirements, or collars. Hedgi abstracts away the technical and financial complexity, making protection accessible to all businesses.
Flexible protection. Today, customers can secure rates for specific time horizons. As the product evolves, we aim to offer open-ended protection for businesses with ongoing, indefinite FX exposures — “always-on currency insurance.”
Seamless access. Hedgi is available via a clean web interface and is designed to function as a plugin for B2B and B2B2C platforms, so companies can embed currency insurance directly where they do business.
Sustainability for business cash flows. By removing friction, Hedgi ensures SMEs can safeguard profits and plan with confidence without needing in-house treasury expertise.
Optimized hedging. As Hedgi grows, we will leverage user data and advanced mathematical modeling to optimize hedging. Further, canceling and rebalancing orders dynamically to secure the best possible execution and lowest fees.
